💡 Essential Tips for the Trip

🚗 Driving in the UK
Left-hand traffic — same as India. Speed limits are in MPH: 30 in towns, 70 on motorways. Always carry IDP + Indian licence + passport when driving.
🚇 London Transport
Get an Oyster card or use contactless bank card — tap in and out at every Tube/bus journey. Much cheaper than buying tickets. The Piccadilly Line is your best friend from Earl's Court.
☀️ May Weather
Expect 12–18°C. Pack layers — a light jacket, a warm mid-layer, and a waterproof shell. May can have brilliant sunny spells but also sudden showers, especially in Scotland.
👴 Seniors & Vedh
Most London museums have lifts and wheelchairs available. Edinburgh Old Town has steep steps — take it slow. Beamish Museum is largely flat and trams help cover the distance. Vedh will need reins or a hand near busy roads.
💷 Money
Most places are contactless. Carry some cash (£50–100) for small shops, tips, and markets like Borough. ATMs are everywhere — use a Wise or Revolut card for best exchange rates.
📱 Connectivity
Buy a UK SIM at the airport (Vodafone / EE). Or activate an international plan before departure. WhatsApp works well for group coordination. Download Google Maps offline for NCL, Edinburgh, and London.
🎟 Pre-Book These
Edinburgh Castle, Scotch Whisky Experience, Tower of London, Legoland Windsor, The Farmer's Dog pub (Chadlington), Gymkhana (London), Ondine (Edinburgh). May is peak season — book 4–6 weeks ahead.
🚄 LNER Train (Day 7)
Book Newcastle → King's Cross in advance at lner.co.uk. Advance fares from ~£35/person. Standard class is perfectly comfortable for a 3-hour trip. Seats at a table are best for the group.
🎵 Live Music Pubs
Newcastle: Crown Posada (The Side, folk/jazz nightly), The Cluny (Ouseburn, eclectic live bands). Edinburgh: Sandy Bell's (Forrest Rd, traditional Scottish folk sessions every night, free). London: The Troubadour (Old Brompton Rd, 7-min from hotel, historic live music venue). All kid-friendly before 9pm.
🥃 Whisky Tips
The Scotch Whisky Experience (Day 5) gives a great overview of all five Scottish whisky regions — Speyside, Highland, Islay, Lowland, Campbeltown. For a bottle to take home, visit Royal Mile Whiskies (379 High St, Edinburgh) — expert staff, huge selection. Ask for an Islay single malt (peaty/smoky) or a Speyside (lighter, fruity).
🦕 Museums (Rainy Day)
If you want a London museum day, Natural History Museum (free, South Kensington) and Science Museum (free, next door) are unmissable — especially for Vedh. British Museum (free, Holborn) has incredible South Asia and India collections. All easily reachable by Tube.
